1. Purpose of the document
The purpose of this style guide is to help localize content for Amazon in a clear, consistent, friendly and polite way for a Norwegian bokmål-speaking audience. Our main goal is to
provide translations that are easy to understand for global customers with an average or low familiarity with Amazon products and services. This appendix consists of language-
specific rules and instructions which should be applied in order to achieve consistent and high-quality translations.
Use this appendix in combination with the Core Style Guide applicable for the project you're working on. While the Core SG provides instructions on how to achieve comparable
stylistic consistency across all languages, this Language Appendix aims to establish consistency for all content translated into your specific language.
Important: if the project you're working on is Light MTPE of ASIN content, only the instructions contained in the ASIN Loc boxes always need to be followed, while the general
instructions in the appendix need to be followed only if not doing so would result in glaring errors.

3.1. Grammar
Hyperlinks and Tags
While copying hyperlinks and tags in the target, make sure to place them in the correct position according to the grammar for Norwegian.
Prepositions
Common preposition errors that you should avoid when translating content:

Place the cursor over… Plasser markøren over… Plasser markøren oppå…

Follow Norwegian capitalization rules.
Capitalize the first letter of titles.

3.3. Typography
Important: for Light MTPE ASIN Loc projects, in case of contradicting rules between the ASIN Loc Core style guide and the language appendix, the Core style guide
should ALWAYS have priority over the language appendix.
Abbreviations and Acronyms
Follow the standard punctuation rules of the target language. If a period is required in Norwegian, please add one. For example mva. is considered an abbreviation that should have
a period according to Språkrådet.
Bullet Points
Use lower case in the bullet points. When the sentences are complete sentences, each bullet point should be capitalized.
English
Norwegian
You can solve many errors by: Du kan løse mange problemer ved å
updating to the latest version of the app oppdatere til den nyeste versjonen av appen.
force stopping the app tvinge appen til å avslutte.
clearing the app cache tømme appens hurtigbuffer.
Try the following: Prøv følgende:

Check that you have the latest version of the app. Sjekk at du har den nyeste versjonen av appen.
Restart your device. Start enheten på nytt.
Dashes
Use an en-dash (–) (key combination Alt+0150) to indicate a range of numbers, a minus sign, or a negative number. There should be no spaces before and after dashes showing
time, distances, relationships or other opposites.
Example:
English Translation
Read pages 32–37. Les side 32–37
Ellipsis
There should be a space both before and after an ellipsis, e.g. "… hos Amazon"
Quotation Marks
Always use double guillemots for quotes. To add the guillemots « » to your translation, use the following key combination: Alt+0171 and Alt+0187.
Place the final punctuation of the sentence where it is grammatically appropriate in Norwegian.
Slashes
As a general rule, there should be no space between the slash and the characters before and after it in Norwegian. If one or both of the expressions separated by the slash contain
several words, a space is used.
Example: ja/nei – kommer / kommer ikke
Titles and headings
There should be no full stops at the end of headings or titles.

4.4. Number Format
Digit separators
Use comma as decimal separator.
Exception: Version numbers (for software etc.) should be written with a period.
Source text (UK English) Correct translation
50.5 50,5
ver. 4.2 ver. 4.2
Use a non-breaking space as thousands separator for numbers 1,000 and higher.
Exception: Numbers used as identifiers, such as model- or part numbers should remain unchanged and retain both the digit grouping and grouping separator they have in the
source.
Source text (UK English) Correct translation
1,526 1 526
1,526,987 1 526 987
L08261-002 L08261-002

Gender -Neutral Language
Translations should be gender neutral whenever it is possible.
Avoid using gendered adjectives if possible, such as kvinnelig and mannlig with job titles (e.g. not kvinnelig sommelier). Also avoid job titles such as kontordame and sykesøster. Use
job titles and job descriptions that include both genders (e.g. sekretær, sykepleier, politibetjent).
Please refer to Språkrådets web site for the latest recommendations on gender. In general, use plural or vedkommende instead of she/he/him/her.

Titles and Headings
English often uses the "ing" forms in titles and section headings in the source text. In Norwegian headlines and titles, the noun form of verbs is usually used, but there is no fixed
rule on how to approach the translation of headings and titles: Choose your translation in accordance to what sounds like the best choice for Norwegian and what makes sense in
the specific context.
Sometimes it will be appropriate to use verbs in the infinitive form, while other times the noun form may be more appropriate to achieve the desired meaning or flow in the
headline.
The most important thing is that the headline is clear, precise and understandable to the target audience.
Abbreviations and Acronyms
To keep our style clear and direct, avoid using abbreviations that might not be understood by everyone.
When you encounter an acronym, always check the term base. If a term has a well-established translation in Norwegian, you should use the Norwegian version.
